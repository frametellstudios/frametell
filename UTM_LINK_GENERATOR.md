## UTM Parameter Builder

### Base URL
Your website: `https://frametell.com`

### UTM Parameters

| Parameter | Purpose | Your Values |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| `utm_source` | Traffic source | `instagram`, `facebook`, `tiktok`, `linkedin`, `google`, `email` |
| `utm_medium` | Marketing medium | `social`, `cpc` (paid ads), `email`, `referral` |
| `utm_campaign` | Campaign name | `wedding_jan2025`, `spring_promo`, `portfolio_showcase` |
| `utm_content` | Content type | `feed_post`, `story`, `reel`, `carousel`, `video_ad` |
| `utm_term` | Keywords (optional) | `wedding_videographer`, `corporate_video` |

## Campaign Naming Conventions

### Best Practices

**Use lowercase:** `wedding_jan2025` not `Wedding_Jan2025`

**Use underscores:** `spring_promo` not `spring-promo` or `spring promo`

**Be specific:** `wedding_portfolio_jan2025` not `portfolio`

**Include date:** `feb2025` or `q1_2025` for time-based campaigns

**Be consistent:** Always use same format

### Good Campaign Names

‚úÖ `wedding_portfolio_jan2025`  
‚úÖ `corporate_video_launch_q1`  
‚úÖ `spring_promo_2025`  
‚úÖ `real_estate_showcase_march`  
‚úÖ `behind_the_scenes_series`

### Bad Campaign Names

‚ùå `Campaign 1`  
‚ùå `test`  
‚ùå `Wedding Portfolio Jan 2025` (spaces, capitals)  
‚ùå `promo` (too vague)

## How to Use These Links

### Step 1: Choose Your Platform

Decide where you're posting: Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, LinkedIn, etc.

### Step 2: Choose Your Campaign

What's the goal of this post?
- Showcase wedding work?
- Promote corporate services?
- Launch new portfolio?
- Run paid ads?

### Step 3: Build Your Link

**Example: Instagram Reel showcasing wedding videography**

1. Base URL: `https://frametell.com/videography`
2. Source: `utm_source=instagram`
3. Medium: `utm_medium=social`
4. Campaign: `utm_campaign=wedding_reel_jan2025`
5. Content: `utm_content=reel`

**Final link:**
```
https://frametell.com/videography?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=wedding_reel_jan2025&utm_content=reel
```

### Step 4: Use the Link

- **Instagram:** Add to bio, or mention "link in bio" in post
- **Facebook:** Include in post text or ad destination
- **TikTok:** Add to bio or comments
- **LinkedIn:** Include in post or article
- **Email:** Use as hyperlink in email content

### Step 5: Track Results

1. Log into Umami Analytics dashboard
2. Go to **Reports** ‚Üí **UTM Parameters**
3. See which campaigns drive traffic and conversions

## Analyzing Your Results

### Weekly Review (15 minutes)

1. Log into Umami dashboard
2. Go to **Reports** ‚Üí **UTM Parameters**
3. Check:
   - Which source drives most traffic? (Instagram vs Facebook vs LinkedIn)
   - Which campaign has best conversion rate?
   - Which content type performs best? (Reels vs posts vs stories)

### Monthly Review (30 minutes)

1. Compare all campaigns side-by-side
2. Calculate ROI for paid campaigns
3. Identify top performers
4. Adjust strategy:
   - Increase budget on high-performing campaigns
   - Pause or optimize low-performing campaigns
   - Double down on best-performing content types

### Example Insights

**Scenario:** After 1 month of tracking

**Data:**
- Instagram: 450 visits, 12 contact forms (2.7% conversion)
- Facebook Ads: 320 visits, 18 contact forms (5.6% conversion)
- LinkedIn: 80 visits, 6 contact forms (7.5% conversion)
- TikTok: 890 visits, 3 contact forms (0.3% conversion)

**Insights:**
- **LinkedIn has highest conversion rate** (7.5%) despite lowest traffic
- **Facebook Ads have good ROI** (5.6% conversion, paid traffic)
- **TikTok drives traffic but not conversions** (awareness only)
- **Instagram is middle ground** (decent traffic and conversions)

**Actions:**
- **Increase LinkedIn activity** (highest quality leads)
- **Maintain Facebook Ads** (good ROI)
- **Use TikTok for brand awareness** (don't expect direct conversions)
- **Optimize Instagram CTAs** (improve conversion rate)

## Tips for Success

### 1. Always Use UTM Links

**Don't:**
- Post: "Check out our website! frametell.com"

**Do:**
- Post: "Link in bio!" (bio has UTM-tagged link)

### 2. Test Different Content Types

Track which content performs best:
- Reels vs feed posts
- Video ads vs image ads
- Stories vs permanent posts

### 3. Be Consistent

Use same naming convention across all campaigns for easy comparison.

### 4. Review Weekly

Don't wait until end of month. Check weekly to adjust quickly.

### 5. Document Everything

Keep a spreadsheet of all campaigns, links, and results.

### 6. A/B Test

Run two versions of same campaign with different content:
- Campaign A: `wedding_jan2025_emotional`
- Campaign B: `wedding_jan2025_cinematic`

See which performs better!

## Troubleshooting

### "My UTM links aren't showing in analytics"

**Check:**
1. Is Umami script installed on website? (Check page source)
2. Did you use correct format? (lowercase, underscores, no spaces)
3. Did anyone actually click the link? (Check social media insights)
4. Is ad blocker blocking Umami? (Test in incognito mode)

### "I see traffic but no conversions"

**Possible reasons:**
1. Traffic is low quality (wrong audience)
2. Landing page doesn't match ad promise
3. No clear call-to-action on landing page
4. Contact form is broken or too complex

**Solutions:**
1. Refine audience targeting
2. Create dedicated landing pages for campaigns
3. Add clear CTAs
4. Test contact form

### "Too many campaigns to track"

**Solution:** Focus on 3-5 main campaigns per month. Quality over quantity.
